Learn with Hyperparameter

We provide top-quality training for data scientists. Our goal is to help you stay at the peak of your ability. We focus on the skills that employers are asking for, and we have the ability to bring you up to speed in new tools and technologies, quickly.

Our courses are highly practical, with short presentations interspersed with practical sessions to reinforce new ideas.

Hyperparameter is founded by Daoud Clarke, a data scientist at Lumi and researcher at the University of Sussex. He has been working in industry for seven years applying machine learning, natural language processing and optimisation techniques to real-world problems, building on knowledge obtained in his PhD in computational linguistics.

Python for Data Scientists

Harness the power of efficient C libraries in a language designed for readability and ease of use. Python, together with numpy, scipy and scikit-learn makes it easy to implement efficient numerical algorithms.

Syllabus:

  • Data science in Python by example
  • Introduction to numpy and scipy arrays and matrices
  • Operator overloading: vector operations in python
  • Tips and tricks: writing concise, efficient code
  • Common pitfalls
  • Introduction to scikit-learn

Machine Learning in Practice

Machine learning is one of the most sought-after skills for data scientists. Used properly, you can unlock hidden patterns in your data that can bring vast benefits to your company in increased efficiency and new revenue. While the basics are easy to learn, it is notoriously difficult to master, being considered somewhat of a black art. We're here to guide you - we'll discuss what's needed to make things work - things that theoretical courses only touch on.

Syllabus:

  • Overview, a first experiment
  • Classification: building a sentiment analysis system. Naive Bayes, SVMs.
  • Clustering: finding patterns in big data. K-means, hierarchical clustering
  • Introduction to recommender systems. Application film recommendations. Singular value decomposition
  • Feature engineering: feature extraction, feature selection, preprocessing
  • The world of machine learning: an overview of important areas and techniques

Date: 21st November 2014
